11.1.1 Properties and Applications of Electromagnetic Waves

Electromagnetic waves are transverse waves that travel with the same speed in a vacuum. They can travel through a vacuum. They do not require any medium to travel from one point to another. 11.1.2 Hazardous Effects of Heating or Ionisation

Over-exposure to electromagnetic waves can have various hazardous effects on living cells and tissues due to heating and ionizing effects.

High-frequency electromagnetic waves, such as X-rays and gamma rays, possess enough energy to ionize atoms and molecules. This ionizing radiation can cause significant damage to living cells and tissues.

  • DNA Damage: Ionizing radiation can directly break DNA strands or create reactive oxygen species (ROS) that damage DNA. This can lead to mutations, cancer, and cell death.
  • Cellular Damage: Ionization can disrupt cellular structures and functions, affecting the cell membrane, proteins, and other essential molecules. This can impair cell signaling and metabolic processes.
  • Acute Radiation Syndrome (ARS): High doses of ionizing radiation can cause ARS, which includes symptoms such as nausea, fatigue, hair loss, skin burns, and, in severe cases, organ failure and death.
